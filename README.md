## The Material Culture of Possible Futures

This repository serves as a functional, diegetic **Field Manual** exploring the four primary idioms of design fiction as delineated by Julian Bleecker and the Near Future Laboratory. It translates theoretical foresight into interactive, material artifacts that can be navigated, examined, and utilized to stress-test the trajectory of human innovation.

### 🌐 The Main Station: `index.html`
The central hub of the project. A brutalist, document-driven interface that grounds the abstract concepts into a tangible format. It features:
*   **The Four Idiom Cards:** Cinematic Prototypes, Fictional Repair Manuals, Speculative Quick Start Guides, and Future Product Catalogs.
*   **Crate Digger Links:** Direct pathways into the specialized, interactive "Crates" built for each idiom.
*   **Grounded Examples:** Real-world case studies (e.g., Boeing MCAS, John Deere Hacks) proving the idioms exist in reality, extracted from our deep research reports.
*   **The Bonus Operators Grid:** A 3-column index of 11 standalone POML prompt architectures for structural pressure simulation and world-building.

### 📦 The Four Idiom Crates

Each idiom has been materialized into a standalone HTML interface reflecting its precise aesthetic and rhetorical function:

1.  **`01-film.html` (The Editor's Bay):** A backlit 35mm slide archive exploring Diegetic Prototypes. Examines how cinema functions as R&D for the future mundane.
2.  **`02-repair.html` (The Workbench):** A grease-stained filing cabinet investigating Fictional Repair Manuals. Focuses on entropy, supply chain failures, and rogue maintenance.
3.  **`03-quick.html` (The Sterile Unboxing):** A pristine, unsettling hospital-white interface exploring Speculative Quick Start Guides. Focuses on onboarding friction, liability shields, and catastrophic user error.
4.  **`04-catalog.html` (The Discount Circular):** A yellowed newsprint flea market examining Future Product Catalogs. Investigates the cheap, plastic commodification of high-tech breakthroughs.

### ⚙️ The POML Operators

The project is driven by a suite of **Prompt Orchestration Markup Language (POML)** engines. 

*   **`diegetic-op.html` & `world-op.html` & `poml-op.html`**: Interactive web applications that act as visual interfaces for executing multi-step POML logic.
*   **`BONUS OPERATORS/`**: A directory containing 11 advanced structural prompt files (e.g., *Octavia Operator*, *Tchaikovsky Gearbox Orchestrator*) designed to enforce rigorous consequence modeling and deny escapist storytelling.

Deep theoretical research backing the interactive components:

*   **`arch-02.md`** & **`site-01.md`**: Comprehensive essays detailing the socio-technical stratification and theoretical underpinning of the four idioms.
*   **`analyst-03.md`** & **`crate-04.md`**: Specialized guidelines and analyses on how to deploy analysts to unpack the implications of the artifacts.
