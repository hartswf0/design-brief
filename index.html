I

🎬 Film — Diegetic Prototypes

08:12
Surviving Core Signals
  • The concept of the "diegetic prototype"
  • Technology must conflict with human behavior
  • The physical looking and functioning of the object
1.0The future cannot be theorized; it must be rendered as a diegetic prototype.
1.1An artifact's validity is proven strictly through its physical friction within a scene.
1.2A technology does not exist cinematically until it visibly conflicts with human behavior.
▽ The Divergent Philogyny

The perfect diegetic prototype is a dead end. To render a future technology that "works seamlessly" is to enforce a boring consensus. The true potential lies in the outtakes — the moments of physical rebellion where the artifact refuses the actor's intent. Do not fix the prop. Do not edit around the jam. Collect the friction.

Branch A · The Biological Toll
What if the device requires a specific biometric stress state to function? The UI doesn't unlock until the user's cortisol spikes. The technology becomes a parasite that farms human anxiety to power its calculations.
Branch B · Symbiotic Hostility
The artifact is redesigned not to serve, but to argue. It physically resists being held. The cinematic frame documents an ongoing, physical wrestling match between human musculature and stubborn, heavy matter.
Branch C · The Outtake Economy
The narrative film is abandoned entirely. We compile a supercut of actors genuinely failing to operate fictional devices. This becomes an instructional manual for future engineers on what not to build.

Interstellar: Gargantua's Missing Doppler Shift (2014)

Her: Los Angeles × Shanghai Pudong Walkways (2013)
Production designer K.K. Barrett fused LA streets with Shanghai's elevated pedestrian infrastructure to create a near-future that felt "around the corner" — avoiding dystopian hardware clichés while embedding a posthumanist relational ontology.

The VFX studio behind Life of Pi filed for bankruptcy 11 days before winning the Academy Award. A late-stage directorial change by Ang Lee forced massive rework at the studio's own expense, destroying their financial viability.

🔧 Repair Manuals

16:16
Surviving Core Signals
  • Advanced technology will inevitably break and fail
  • The absolute requirement of an infrastructure of maintenance
2.0Entropy is the baseline condition of all advanced technology.
2.1The reality of a speculative object is authenticated exclusively by its capacity to break.
2.2A design fiction is incomplete until its shadow infrastructure of maintenance and repair is codified.
▽ The Divergent Philogyny

To seek "repair" is to suffer from the Objective Fallacy. Returning an object to its original state deletes its history and erases its accumulated potential. The baseline condition of entropy is not a problem to be solved; it is an engine of unguided mutation. We must abandon the OEM specifications. The margin notes are the only text that matters now.

Branch A · Intentional Degradation Architecture
We stop engineering for durability and start engineering for spectacular, fertile decay. We design machines whose components rot into useful secondary chemicals, creating an ecosystem where "breaking" is the only way to harvest the next generation of tools.
Branch B · The Shadow Economy of the Chimera
The original intended function is forgotten. The "repair" infrastructure becomes a vast, decentralized art movement where mechanics combine broken components from disparate industries to build monstrous, single-use kinetic sculptures.
Branch C · The Manual of Sabotage
The document ceases to be a guide for fixing and becomes a grimoire for intentional breaking. It catalogs exactly which wires to cross to make the machine hallucinate, sing, or permanently forget its primary programming.

III

📋 Quick Start Guides

25:03
Surviving Core Signals
  • Explaining complex systems through mundane instructions
  • Confrontation with everyday scenarios: sanitizing, retrieving lost items
3.0The complexity of a hypothetical system is anchored by the banality of its onboarding.
3.1Interface design must anticipate and codify mundane human failure.
3.2The blueprint of the future is found in the protocols for cleaning up after strangers and retrieving lost items.
▽ The Divergent Philogyny

Clarity is a trap. A guide that flawlessly instructs a user ensures the product will only ever do one boring thing. True potential requires the injection of ambiguity. The banality of onboarding must be weaponized to harvest aberrant human behaviors. We must stop trying to correct the user's mistakes and start building infrastructure to support their beautiful misunderstandings.

Branch A · The Nomadic Safe Deposit Box
The error becomes the feature. Vehicles are no longer designed for passengers. They are hired to drive valuable physical objects endlessly around a city grid at 15mph, making the objects impossible to steal, seize, or locate without the dynamic encryption key.
Branch B · The Rorschach Interface
The Quick Start Guide is deliberately printed with optical illusions and contradictory steps. It is designed to act as a psychological diagnostic tool. How a user attempts to turn the machine on reveals their hidden neuroses, which the machine then records and categorizes.
Branch C · The Theology of the Cleanup Protocol
The mundane tasks of "sanitizing the vehicle" and "resetting the system" evolve into highly ritualized, algorithmic superstitions among gig-workers. The manual morphs into a sacred text where the literal meaning is lost, but the physical repetition of the wiping gestures becomes a form of moving meditation.

🏷️ Product Catalogs

38:57
Surviving Core Signals
  • Advanced innovations becoming ordinary commodities
  • Complex technologies normalized to the level of an umbrella or flashlight
4.0The terminal velocity of all innovation is hyper-commodification.
4.1A technological miracle is only fully realized once it has been reduced to discount retail.
4.2The future is authenticated by its exact economic equivalence to a cheap flashlight.
▽ The Divergent Philogyny

The roadmap is a graveyard of predictability. To wait for technology to "mature" is to miss the fertile chaos of the gray market. We must abandon the pursuit of the "miracle" and dive headfirst into the muck of the 99-cent hyper-commodity. The junk drawer is the only true laboratory left. We collect the cheap, the plastic, and the poorly translated.

Branch A · The Throwaway Array
Society stops building permanent infrastructure. Instead, millions of 99-cent, disposable algorithmic sensors are dumped out of airplanes like confetti. They form fragile, temporary mesh networks that operate for 48 hours before dissolving in the rain.
Branch B · The Catalog of Reductions
Innovation pivots from adding features to selling the localized deletion of them. Blister packs sold at gas stations contain devices that emit a 5-foot radius of total Wi-Fi dead zones, or perfectly silence the ambient hum of electricity for exactly ten minutes before shorting out.
Branch C · Pure Aesthetic Circulation
The utility of the technology hits absolute zero. The dollar store catalog becomes a gallery of empty plastic shells with LED lights that do nothing but blink in novel patterns. The economy is sustained entirely by the dopamine hit of purchasing the shape of the future, unburdened by the need for it to actually work.
